Industrial systems fail unpredictably, costing significant revenue in unplanned downtime~\cite{mckinsey2023}. Predictive Maintenance (PdM) leverages sensor data to forecast equipment failures before they occur, enabling proactive interventions that reduce costs and improve safety. Recent advances in Time Series Foundation Models (TSFMs)---large models pretrained on internet-scale temporal data---promise zero-shot transfer across domains without task-specific training. Models such as MOMENT~\cite{goswami2024moment}, Chronos~\cite{ansari2024chronos}, and Lag-Llama~\cite{rasul2024lagllama} have demonstrated strong performance on standard forecasting benchmarks.

However, industrial PdM poses unique challenges that are absent from standard academic benchmarks:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Non-IID distributions}: Sensor data varies significantly across machines, operating conditions, and deployment sites.
    \item \textbf{Sparse failure labels}: Equipment failures are rare events, creating severe class imbalance that challenges supervised learning approaches.
    \item \textbf{Multi-variate sensor fusion}: Modern industrial systems generate data from dozens of correlated sensors.
    \item \textbf{Cross-condition variability}: Models trained or calibrated on one operating condition may degrade under different loads or fault modes.
\end{itemize}

Recent work has begun evaluating TSFMs in this space. FoundTS~\cite{foundts2024} and TSFM-Bench provide broad TSFM comparisons, while Dinten and Zorrilla~\cite{dinten2025} specifically evaluate TSFMs on C-MAPSS RUL prediction. Our work contributes a complementary, reproducible evaluation focused on open-source models across a wider set of industrial fault scenarios.

\subsection{Contributions}

This paper makes three contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Empirical benchmark} of three open-source TSFMs and one supervised baseline on three public industrial PdM datasets, covering zero-shot and few-shot evaluation scenarios.
    \item \textbf{Cross-condition transfer study} measuring TSFM generalization across the four C-MAPSS operating condition subsets (FD001--FD004).
    \item \textbf{Deployment efficiency analysis} comparing model size, GPU memory requirements, and inference latency to characterize suitability for edge versus cloud PdM deployments.
\end{enumerate}

All code, preprocessing scripts, and experiment configurations are released publicly to support reproducibility.
