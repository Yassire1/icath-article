Time Series Foundation Models (TSFMs) promise zero-shot generalization across domains, yet their efficacy in industrial Predictive Maintenance (PdM) remains largely uncharacterized. We benchmark three leading open-source TSFMs (MOMENT, Chronos, Lag-Llama) against the supervised PatchTST baseline across three public PdM datasets spanning wind turbines, turbofan engines, and factory machinery. We evaluate zero-shot and few-shot performance on forecasting and anomaly detection tasks, with a cross-condition transfer study on turbofan degradation data.

\textbf{Key contributions:} (1) An empirical comparison of zero-shot and few-shot TSFM performance on industrial sensor data; (2) A cross-condition transfer evaluation measuring how well TSFMs generalize across different operating conditions within the same domain; (3) Practical deployment guidelines quantifying model efficiency trade-offs (parameters, VRAM, inference latency) relevant for edge and cloud PdM deployments. Code and datasets available at: \url{https://github.com/YassireF/tsfm-industrial-bench}.
